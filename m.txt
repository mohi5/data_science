    <span style="font-size:12pt;font-weight:bold">Using Control-F/Command-F to
    find a word on the page</span></li>

    <li style=
    "margin-right:0;color:#000000;direction:ltr;font-size:11pt;margin-left:36pt;margin-bottom:0;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;;margin-top:0;padding:0">
    <span style="font-size:12pt;font-weight:bold">Locating the
    &ldquo;find&rdquo; box in different browsers</span></li>
  </ol>

  <p style=
  "height:11pt;color:#000000;direction:ltr;font-size:11pt;margin:0;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;;padding:0">

  <p style=
  "color:#000000;direction:ltr;font-size:11pt;margin:0;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;;padding:0">
  <span style="font-size:12pt">Have you ever needed</span> <span style=
  "font-size:12pt">to locate words on a page efficiently? Suppose you want to
  find your time in the local 8K race. It&rsquo;s easy to find the page that
  contains the information, but what do you do then? &nbsp;</span></p>

  <p style=
  "height:11pt;color:#000000;direction:ltr;font-size:11pt;margin:0;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;;padding:0">
  </p>

  <p style=
  "color:#000000;direction:ltr;font-size:11pt;margin:0;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;;padding:0">
  <span style="font-size:12pt">One option is the Find feature of your web
  browser, using Control-F (Command-F on Mac), to find a piece of text on a web
  page. Unlike searching, which looks across a number of web pages to find
  which of the pages contain certain words,</span> <span style=
  "font-size:12pt">Control-F/Command-F l</span><span style=
  "font-size:12pt">ooks within the text of a single page to find precisely
  where that word is located.</span></p>

  <p style=
  "height:11pt;color:#000000;direction:ltr;font-size:11pt;margin:0;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;;padding:0">
  </p>

  <p style=
  "color:#000000;direction:ltr;font-size:11pt;margin:0;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;;padding:0">
  <span style="font-size:12pt">You can try out Control-F/Command-F on any web
  page. For example, if you want to know where Mumbai, India ranks among the
  most populous cities in the world, you might search for [</span><span style=
  "color:#1155cc;font-size:12pt;text-decoration:underline"><a href=
  "https://www.google.com/search?sugexp=chrome,mod=11&amp;sourceid=chrome&amp;ie=UTF-8&amp;q=most+populous+cities+world"
  style="color:inherit;text-decoration:inherit">most populous cities
  world</a></span><span style="font-size:12pt">] and pick one of the pages that
  come up. Next, your task is to locate the word</span> <span style=
  "font-style:italic;font-size:12pt">Mumbai</span><span style=
  "font-size:12pt">&nbsp;on the page.</span></p>

  <p style=
  "height:11pt;color:#000000;direction:ltr;font-size:11pt;margin:0;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;;padding:0">
  </p>

  <p style=
  "color:#000000;direction:ltr;font-size:11pt;margin:0;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;;padding:0">
  <span style="font-size:12pt">Find the Control key (Command key on Mac). While
  holding that key down, also press the</span> <span style=
  "font-style:italic;font-size:12pt">F</span><span style=
  "font-size:12pt">&nbsp;key. &nbsp;A</span> <span style=
  "font-style:italic;font-size:12pt">find</span><span style=
  "font-size:12pt">&nbsp;box will open, and you can enter the word</span>
  <span style="font-style:italic;font-size:12pt">Mumbai</span><span style=
  "font-size:12pt">&nbsp;to locate it on the page.</span></p>

  <p style=
  "height:11pt;color:#000000;direction:ltr;font-size:11pt;margin:0;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;;padding:0">
  </p>

  <p style=
  "color:#000000;direction:ltr;font-size:11pt;margin:0;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;;padding:0">
  <span style="font-size:12pt">Where exactly your find box opens on the screen
  varies, depending on which browser you use:</span></p>

  <p style=
  "height:11pt;color:#000000;direction:ltr;font-size:11pt;margin:0;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;;padding:0">
  </p>

  <ol start="1" style="list-style-type:disc;margin:0;padding:0">
    <li style=
    "margin-right:0;color:#000000;direction:ltr;font-size:11pt;margin-left:36pt;margin-bottom:0;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;;margin-top:0;padding:0">
    <span style="font-size:12pt;font-weight:bold">Firefox:</span> <span style=
    "font-size:12pt">B</span><span style="font-size:12pt">ottom left corner of
    the screen. You can type in the word you want to find and use the</span>
    <span style="font-style:italic;font-size:12pt">next</span><span style=
    "font-size:12pt">&nbsp;and</span> <span style=
    "font-style:italic;font-size:12pt">previous</span> <span style=
    "font-size:12pt">buttons to go forward or backward in the document. You can
    click on the button that says "highlight all" and that puts a yellow
    background on all instances of that text anywhere in the document.
    &nbsp;</span></li>

    <li style=
    "margin-right:0;color:#000000;direction:ltr;font-size:11pt;margin-left:36pt;margin-bottom:0;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;;margin-top:0;padding:0">
    <span style="font-size:12pt;font-weight:bold">Safari:</span> <span style=
    "font-size:12pt">U</span><span style="font-size:12pt">pper right corner of
    the screen. If you type a word on this page, you can see how many matches
    there are, and you can also use forward and back buttons. You can use the
    "Enter" ("return" on Mac) key to go forward to the next search as well.
    &nbsp;</span></li>

    <li style=
    "margin-right:0;color:#000000;direction:ltr;font-size:11pt;margin-left:36pt;margin-bottom:0;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;;margin-top:0;padding:0">
    <span style="font-size:12pt;font-weight:bold">Internet
    Explorer:</span><span style="font-size:12pt">&nbsp;In IE 9, the box is in
    the upper left corner.</span></li>

    <li style=
    "margin-right:0;color:#000000;direction:ltr;font-size:11pt;margin-left:36pt;margin-bottom:0;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;;margin-top:0;padding:0">
    <span style="font-size:12pt;font-weight:bold">Chrome:</span> <span style=
    "font-size:12pt">U</span><span style="font-size:12pt">pper right corner of
    the screen. You can see how many instances of that word there are.
    &nbsp;</span></li>

    <li style=
    "margin-right:0;color:#000000;direction:ltr;font-size:11pt;margin-left:36pt;margin-bottom:0;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;;margin-top:0;padding:0">
    <span style="font-size:12pt;font-weight:bold">iPad
    (Safari):</span><span style="font-size:12pt">&nbsp;When you are viewing a
    web page, click on the search box in the upper right corner of the screen.
    The find box appears on the top right side of the on-screen
    keyboard.</span></li>

    <li style=
    "margin-right:0;color:#000000;direction:ltr;font-size:11pt;margin-left:36pt;margin-bottom:0;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;;margin-top:0;padding:0">
    <span style="font-size:12pt;font-weight:bold">Android
    tablet:</span><span style="font-size:12pt">&nbsp;</span><span style=
    "font-size:12pt;background-color:#ffffff">Select menu in top right, then
    select "Find on page" in the menu.</span></li>
  </ol>

  <p style=
  "height:11pt;color:#000000;direction:ltr;font-size:11pt;margin:0;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;;padding:0">

  <p style=
  "color:#000000;direction:ltr;font-size:11pt;margin:0;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;;padding:0">
  <span style="font-size:12pt">This feature allows you to review a long
  document quickly. &nbsp;</span></p>

  <p style=
  "height:11pt;color:#000000;direction:ltr;font-size:11pt;margin:0;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;;padding:0">
  </p>

  <p style=
  "color:#000000;direction:ltr;font-size:11pt;margin:0;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;;padding:0">
  <span style="font-size:12pt">When you use the Find feature, consider what
  word to use. For example, assume you want to know which games Scotland played
  in cricket in the month of August 2010. If you have a</span> <span style=
  "color:#1155cc;font-size:12pt;text-decoration:underline"><a href=
  "http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/August_2010_in_sports" style=
  "color:inherit;text-decoration:inherit">list of sporting events in August
  2010</a></span><span style="font-size:12pt">&nbsp;and use the Find feature to
  look for [scotland], you would need to scroll through 26 occurrences of the
  term to find the ones related to cricket. Alternatively, you might choose to
  look for the word [cricket] on the page. There are 29 occurrences of
  cricket,</span> <span style="font-size:12pt">but since the word "cricket" is
  always at the head of each section, it might be a faster process.</span></p>

  <p style=
  "height:11pt;color:#000000;direction:ltr;font-size:11pt;margin:0;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;;padding:0">
  </p>

  <p style=
  "color:#000000;direction:ltr;font-size:11pt;margin:0;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;;padding:0">
  <span style="font-size:12pt">Please note that at this time, there is no easy
  way to use Control-F/ Command-F to find two words near each other if they are
  not adjacent. &nbsp;</span></p>

  <p style=
  "height:11pt;color:#000000;direction:ltr;font-size:11pt;margin:0;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;;padding:0">
  </p>

  <p style=
  "color:#000000;direction:ltr;font-size:11pt;margin:0;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;;padding:0">
  <span style="font-size:12pt">Why do you need to know this? The big reason is
  that it can really speed up finding what you want. In our studies we've found
  that it cuts the time people need to locate information in regular web search
  by about 12%. When we carried out this study, we found that only 10% of all
  searchers knew about using Control-F/Command-F.</span></p>
